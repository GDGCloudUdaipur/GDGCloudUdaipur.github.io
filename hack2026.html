TECHNICAL BRIEFING (FAQ)

Google Lakecity Hackathon 2026 is open to individuals and teams who are enthusiastic about building technology-driven solutions. This includes students (undergraduates and postgraduates), working professionals, hobbyist developers, and early-stage startups. Teams should consist of 1–3 members. There is no age limit to participate.
Register for the event through the official GDG event page (click any “Register Now” button on this page). After registering, please join the WhatsApp group for real-time updates and community support. You will receive a confirmation message and timely instructions for preparing and submitting your Round 1 materials before the deadline on 30 Jan 2026.
For Round 1, include: a fully working prototype demonstrated via YouTube video (max 3 mins), a Google Slides deck (6–10 slides) covering the problem, solution, architecture, and tech stack, and a short elevator pitch video.
There are two rounds: Round 1 (Online Online Submission), and the Final Round (In-person) held in Udaipur. Round 1 evaluates submissions and selects finalists for the offline final where winners are determined.
Round 1 (Online) requires a fully functioning prototype, a 3-minute YouTube demo, a slide deck, and a short elevator pitch. Finalists invited to the in-person final round should prepare a live demonstration, a deeper technical walkthrough, and be ready for Q&A.
Judges will evaluate submissions using a framework that includes: Innovation, Technical Execution (Functional Prototype), Impact (Measurable Benefit), Feasibility, UX & Design, and effective use of Google Tech.
